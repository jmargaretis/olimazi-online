# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Olimazi is for people evaluating what one person can build with AI: prospective collaborators, small operators, tracker testers, and other learners who want evidence and context rather than abstract claims. They arrive at a public personal-brand site to understand the work, the experience behind it, and what is still being learned.

## Product Purpose

Olimazi presents a personal concept: practical experiments shaped by real experience in tax operations, restaurant operations, and building Method Effects. It exists to show working artifacts, explain the context behind them, and document what works and what does not. Success means a visitor can quickly understand the person, the proof, and the current builds without mistaking the site for a tools platform or a finished product company.

## Brand Personality

Candid, practical, and curious. The voice is personal and specific, confident about completed work, honest about work in progress, and more interested in useful evidence than sales polish.

## Anti-references

- A tools platform or finished product company.
- A subscription-bookkeeping SaaS sales page.
- A pitch-led AI landing page that substitutes claims for working evidence.
- A polished success story that hides constraints, uncertainty, or unfinished learning.

## Design Principles

1. **Proof over pitch.** Lead with real work, screenshots, artifacts, and outcomes.
2. **Context first.** Explain the lived problem and prior experience before presenting the tool.
3. **Show the learning.** Keep finished work, current experiments, and open questions distinct.
4. **Keep ownership visible.** When presenting the finance tracker, emphasize local files, portable records, and deterministic math.
5. **Stay personal.** Olimazi connects one person’s work, interests, and operating experience; it is not a generic AI brand.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Preserve the existing keyboard-accessible navigation, visible focus treatment, skip link, semantic headings, image alt text, and native controls. Continue supporting reduced-motion preferences, light and dark themes, and layouts down to the current 320px minimum width. Do not communicate status or meaning through color alone.
