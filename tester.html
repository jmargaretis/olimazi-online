Rental Manager Sch. E

Your rental books already have a life. Let’s make them easier to finish.

Rent comes in by Zelle. The plumber got paid half in cash. A hardware-store receipt is still in the truck. Then spring arrives and you rebuild the whole year from bank statements.

I’m looking for a few self-managing rental owners to test a simpler way to keep those records together.

A working prototype for 1–5 properties. No account. No cloud. No subscription.

Built for a particular kind of owner.

This may be for you if…

  • You manage one to five rental properties yourself.
  • You want clearer books without giving up control of your files.
  • You currently use spreadsheets, folders, notes, or a mix of all three.

It is not for…

  • Large portfolios or property managers already using full management software.
  • Anyone looking for tax advice or a tax-return service.
  • Someone who wants every record stored in an online account.

One local place for the year’s loose ends.

Rental Manager (Sch. E) is a local-file organizer. It brings income, expenses, notes, and still-unresolved items into one dashboard. The calculations follow fixed rules, so the same inputs produce the same totals. Anything that still needs an answer stays visible instead of being quietly guessed.

  1. Files stay on your machine. Your working records remain portable and under your control.
  2. The math stays predictable. Totals come from clear rules, not generated estimates.
  3. Open questions stay open. Missing categories, unclear payments, and follow-ups remain on the dashboard until you resolve them.

Testing means using it, then checking in.

You can load your own records or start with the included sample. Use Rental Manager for a few weeks, then answer short, structured check-ins at useful moments. I’m looking for specific feedback: what imported cleanly, what was confusing, where you got stuck, and whether the final files made sense.

  1. First runLoad the sample or your own records and report setup problems.
  2. Working check-inUse the dashboard during normal bookkeeping and flag unclear steps.
  3. End-of-test reviewConfirm what helped, what failed, and what would make the organizer worth keeping.

Your books stay yours.

There is no cloud service and no account to create. Rental Manager works with files on your machine. I never see your books unless you choose to share a redacted screenshot—one with names, addresses, account numbers, and other identifying details removed.

This is an organizer, not tax advice. Confirm figures and tax treatment with your tax preparer.
